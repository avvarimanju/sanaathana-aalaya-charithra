# Content Sources for AI Generation

This document specifies the authoritative sources that should be used for generating content about Hindu temples and artifacts in the Sanaathana Aalaya Charithra application.

## Primary Sources

### Archaeological & Government Sources
1. **Archaeological Survey of India (ASI)**
   - Official website: https://asi.nic.in
   - Temple documentation and historical records
   - Conservation reports

2. **Ministry of Culture, Government of India**
   - Official website: https://www.indiaculture.gov.in
   - Cultural heritage documentation

3. **State Archaeological Departments**
   - Andhra Pradesh State Archaeology
   - Karnataka State Archaeology
   - Tamil Nadu State Archaeology

### Religious & Temple Authorities
4. **Tirumala Tirupati Devasthanams (TTD)**
   - Official website: https://www.tirumala.org
   - Authoritative source for Tirumala Temple

5. **Endowments Department**
   - State-specific temple endowment departments
   - Official temple records and histories

### Academic & Research Sources
6. **Indian Council of Historical Research (ICHR)**
   - Peer-reviewed historical research

7. **Universities & Research Institutions**
   - Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) heritage studies
   - National Institute of Advanced Studies (NIAS)
   - Centre for Cultural Resources and Training (CCRT)

### Traditional Texts & Scriptures
8. **Agama Shastras**
   - Temple architecture and rituals
   - Traditional knowledge systems

9. **Sthala Puranas**
   - Temple-specific historical texts
   - Legendary and mythological accounts

10. **Inscriptions (Shilashasanas)**
    - Stone inscriptions at temple sites
    - Historical records carved in stone

## Secondary Sources

### Books & Publications
- "The Hindu Temple" by Stella Kramrisch
- "South Indian Temples" by K.R. Srinivasan
- "Vijayanagara Architecture" by George Michell
- Publications by Indian National Trust for Art and Cultural Heritage (INTACH)

### Digital Archives
- Digital Library of India
- National Mission for Manuscripts
- Google Arts & Culture (India Heritage section)

## Content Generation Guidelines

When generating content using AI:

1. **Prioritize Primary Sources**: Always prefer information from ASI, temple authorities, and government sources

2. **Cross-Reference**: Verify information across multiple sources when possible

3. **Cite Sources**: Include source attribution in generated content metadata

4. **Respect Tradition**: Ensure content respects religious sentiments and traditional beliefs

5. **Accuracy Over Speculation**: Stick to documented facts; clearly mark legends/traditions as such

6. **Language Sensitivity**: Use appropriate Sanskrit/regional language terms with translations

7. **Avoid Controversial Topics**: Focus on architecture, art, history, and cultural significance

## Sources to Avoid

- Wikipedia (use only for initial research, not as primary source)
- Personal blogs without credentials
- Social media posts
- Unverified tourist websites
- Sources with political or religious bias

## Content Review Process

All AI-generated content should be:
1. Reviewed by subject matter experts
2. Cross-checked with primary sources
3. Approved by temple authorities (where applicable)
4. Updated regularly as new research emerges

## Source Attribution Format

When storing content in the database, include:
```json
{
  "content": "...",
  "sources": [
    {
      "type": "primary",
      "name": "Archaeological Survey of India",
      "reference": "ASI Report No. XXX",
      "url": "https://asi.nic.in/...",
      "accessDate": "2026-02-27"
    }
  ],
  "verifiedBy": "Expert Name/Organization",
  "lastUpdated": "2026-02-27"
}
```
